About the Thesis Archive

The Thesis Archive Management System is a curated digital library for student research. It keeps every approved thesis organized, searchable, and ready for future study. Built for students, advisers, and librarians, it streamlines submission, review, and preservation from proposal to final archive.

What We Protect

  • Verified authorship and academic integrity
  • Complete submission history with revisions
  • Long-term access to institutional research

Purpose

Provide a trusted repository where theses are preserved, discoverable, and categorized by department, year, and keywords.

How It Works

Students submit their work, advisers and panels validate it, and the library team archives the final copy for campus access.

Who It Serves

Students looking for references, advisers reviewing progress, and librarians maintaining the university research legacy.

Data Stewardship

Version history, permissions, and secure storage ensure that every document stays intact and traceable over time.

Discovery

Advanced search, tags, and category views help students find relevant studies across disciplines quickly.

Impact

By reusing prior research, students build stronger proposals and prevent duplicated work across batches.
